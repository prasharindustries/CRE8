Fixing How Creators Get Paid

CRE8 is a decentralized payment protocol designed to fix one of the most broken systems on the internet: how creators get paid. Today, creators generate billions in value, yet they don’t control how or when they get paid. Platforms act as intermediaries, taking a significant cut while delaying payouts by weeks or even months.

CRE8 replaces this with programmable, instant payments. Using smart contracts, revenue is automatically split and distributed the moment a transaction occurs—no intermediaries, no delays, no ambiguity.

We’re not building another creator platform. We’re building the financial infrastructure that powers the next generation of them.

Why CRE8 Exists

The creator economy is projected to exceed hundreds of billions in value, yet its financial infrastructure remains outdated and centralized. While content distribution has evolved rapidly, payment systems have not. Creators still rely on platforms that control monetization, enforce arbitrary payout cycles, and lack transparency in revenue distribution. Even within Web3, most solutions fail to solve the core problem. They focus on ownership (NFTs, tokens) but ignore payment execution. Revenue distribution is still manual, delayed, or dependent on off-chain coordination. This creates friction, reduces trust, and limits scalability in collaborative environments. CRE8 addresses this gap by introducing a decentralized payment layer specifically designed for creators. It automates revenue sharing at the protocol level, ensuring that funds are distributed instantly and deterministically based on predefined logic. This is not an incremental improvement—it is a structural shift. By removing intermediaries and embedding trust into code, CRE8 enables a more efficient, scalable, and creator-first economy.

  • Transparent revenue flow

How It Works

CRE8 is built as a decentralized application on the Polygon blockchain, leveraging Solidity smart contracts to automate financial workflows. The core innovation lies in programmable revenue distribution—payments are split and executed automatically at the transaction level, eliminating the need for manual processing or third-party intervention. The current implementation demonstrates a simple but powerful use case: automatic revenue splitting between a creator and a collaborator. However, the underlying architecture is modular and extensible, capable of supporting multi-party distributions, dynamic percentages, subscription-based payouts, and integration with existing platforms. From a technical standpoint, CRE8 integrates Ethers.js for blockchain communication and MetaMask for wallet authentication, ensuring seamless interaction for users. The system is lightweight, scalable, and designed for real-world deployment. What differentiates CRE8 is its focus on execution, not just ownership. While most Web3 projects focus on tokenization, CRE8 focuses on cash flow—the actual movement of money. This positions it as critical infrastructure rather than a feature, making it applicable across platforms rather than tied to a single ecosystem.

Live Demo

The live demo showcases CRE8’s core functionality: real-time, automated revenue distribution using smart contracts. Users can connect their wallet, input participant addresses, and execute a transaction that is instantly split and distributed according to predefined logic. This demonstration validates the system’s ability to eliminate delays and enforce transparent financial coordination between multiple parties. Every transaction is recorded on-chain, providing full visibility and auditability. More importantly, the demo highlights the simplicity of the user experience. Despite being powered by complex blockchain infrastructure, the interaction is intuitive and requires no deep technical knowledge. This is critical for adoption, as usability remains a major barrier in Web3. CRE8 is currently a proof-of-concept, but it establishes a strong foundation for scaling into a full protocol layer for creator payments. The next steps involve expanding functionality, improving UX, and integrating with platforms where creators already operate.
